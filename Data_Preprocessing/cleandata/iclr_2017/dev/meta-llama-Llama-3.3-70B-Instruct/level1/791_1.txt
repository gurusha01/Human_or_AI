Summary of the Paper's Claims and Contributions
The paper presents a novel approach for unsupervised training of convolutional networks, called spatial contrasting, which is based on comparing spatial regions within images. The authors claim that this approach can improve the performance of convolutional networks on subsequent supervised training tasks, especially when a large amount of unlabeled data is available. The paper also provides empirical results on several datasets, including STL10, CIFAR10, and MNIST, demonstrating the effectiveness of the spatial contrasting criterion in improving classification accuracy.
