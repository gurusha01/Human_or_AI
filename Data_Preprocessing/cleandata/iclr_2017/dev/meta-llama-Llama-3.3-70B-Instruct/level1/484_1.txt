The paper "Inductive Bias of Deep Convolutional Networks through Pooling Geometry" presents a theoretical analysis of the inductive bias of deep convolutional networks, specifically focusing on the role of pooling geometry in controlling the bias. The authors introduce the concept of separation rank, which measures the correlation between different regions of the input, and show that the pooling geometry of a deep network determines which input partitions are favored in terms of separation rank.
The paper claims to contribute to the understanding of the inductive bias of deep convolutional networks by providing a theoretical framework for analyzing the effect of pooling geometry on the correlations modeled by the network. The authors demonstrate that a polynomially sized deep network can support exponentially high separation ranks for certain input partitions, while being limited to polynomial or linear separation ranks for others.
