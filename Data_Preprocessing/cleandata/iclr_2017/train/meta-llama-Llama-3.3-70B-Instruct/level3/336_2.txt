This paper proposes a novel online dictionary learning approach, called Neurogenetic Online Dictionary Learning (NODL), which extends the state-of-art online dictionary learning method to non-stationary environments. The key idea is to incorporate online model adaptation by adding and deleting dictionary elements in response to changes in the input data distribution. The approach is inspired by adult neurogenesis, a process in the brain where new neurons are born and old ones die, allowing for improved cognitive function and adaptation to new environments.
The paper claims to contribute to the field of online representation learning by proposing a new approach that can adapt to non-stationary environments, outperforming the standard online dictionary learning method in certain settings. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on various real-life datasets, including images and language data, as well as on synthetic data.
