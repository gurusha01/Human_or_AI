Summary of the Paper's Contributions
The paper presents a novel approach to understanding the Hessian of the loss function in deep learning, revealing a two-phased spectrum with a bulk of eigenvalues concentrated around zero and a discrete part that depends on the data. The authors demonstrate this phenomenon through empirical experiments on various datasets and network architectures, providing insights into the implications of this discovery on the theoretical and practical aspects of deep learning.
