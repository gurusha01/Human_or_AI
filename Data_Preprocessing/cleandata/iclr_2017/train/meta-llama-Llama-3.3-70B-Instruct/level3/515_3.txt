Summary of the Paper's Contributions
The paper proposes a novel approach to detecting misclassified or out-of-distribution examples using softmax prediction probabilities. The authors demonstrate that these probabilities can be used as a baseline for detection, and they provide a simple yet effective method for detecting errors and out-of-distribution examples across various tasks and architectures. Additionally, the authors introduce an abnormality module that can be used to improve detection performance by exploiting the internal representations of neural networks.
Decision and Key Reasons
Based on the review, I decide to Accept the paper. The two key reasons for this decision are:
1. The paper tackles a specific and well-motivated problem, namely detecting misclassified or out-of-distribution examples, which is an important issue in machine learning.
2. The authors provide a clear and well-supported argument for their approach, including empirical experiments that demonstrate the effectiveness of their method.
