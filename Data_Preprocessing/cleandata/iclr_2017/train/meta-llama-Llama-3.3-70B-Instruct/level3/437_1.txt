This paper proposes the Generative Multi-Adversarial Network (GMAN), a framework that extends Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) to multiple discriminators. The authors introduce a number of design perspectives, ranging from a formidable adversary to a forgiving teacher, and explore the benefits of using multiple discriminators in the GAN framework.
The paper claims to contribute to the field of GANs by providing a framework that allows for more robust and efficient training of generators. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through a variety of image generation tasks, including MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CelebA.
Based on the provided information, I decide to accept this paper. The key reasons for this choice are:
1. The paper tackles a specific and well-defined problem in the field of GANs, namely the difficulty of training generators in the presence of a single discriminator.
