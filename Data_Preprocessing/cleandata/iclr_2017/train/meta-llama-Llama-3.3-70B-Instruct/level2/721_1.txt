Summary of the Paper
The paper proposes a novel approach to unsupervised learning, called Transformational Sparse Coding (TSC), which learns object features jointly with their transformations from data. The authors argue that current deep learning methods and sparse coding techniques have limitations in achieving invariance to local translations and transformations. TSC uses a tree structure to represent common transformations in data, allowing the model to learn features and transformations simultaneously. The approach is shown to match the reconstruction quality of traditional sparse coding with significantly fewer degrees of freedom.
Decision
I decide to Accept this paper, with the main reason being that the approach is well-motivated and supported by experiments. The paper tackles a specific problem in computer vision, namely learning object features and transformations jointly, and provides a novel solution that is consistent with the concept of "capsules" and the dorsal-ventral stream architecture of the primate visual cortex.
Supporting Arguments
The paper provides a clear and well-structured introduction to the problem, and the proposed approach is well-motivated by the limitations of current methods. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of TSC in learning features and transformations from natural image patches, and the comparison with traditional sparse coding shows that TSC achieves comparable reconstruction quality with fewer degrees of freedom. The paper also provides a thorough discussion of related work and highlights the advantages of TSC over existing approaches.
Additional Feedback
To further improve the paper, I suggest that the authors provide more details on the optimization process, particularly on how the transformation parameters are learned and how the tree structure is constructed. Additionally, it would be interesting to see more examples of learned features and transformations, and to explore the application of TSC to more complex datasets and tasks. The authors may also consider discussing the potential limitations of TSC and how they can be addressed in future work.
Questions for the Authors
1. Can you provide more details on the optimization process, particularly on how the transformation parameters are learned and how the tree structure is constructed?
