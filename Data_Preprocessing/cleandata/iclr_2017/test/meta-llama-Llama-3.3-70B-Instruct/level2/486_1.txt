This paper presents a novel approach for semi-supervised learning on graph-structured data using a scalable and efficient variant of convolutional neural networks. The authors introduce a simple and well-behaved layer-wise propagation rule for neural network models that operate directly on graphs, which is motivated by a first-order approximation of spectral graph convolutions. The model is shown to outperform several recently proposed methods by a significant margin on a number of network datasets, while being computationally efficient.
The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method, including the theoretical motivation and the experimental setup. The results are impressive, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the performance of their model on different datasets.
