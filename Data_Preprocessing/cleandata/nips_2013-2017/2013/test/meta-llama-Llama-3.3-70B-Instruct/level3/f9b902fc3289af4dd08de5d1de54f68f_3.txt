This paper proposes a novel approach to learning stochastic And-Or grammars, which are powerful models for representing patterns that combine compositionality and reconfigurability. The authors present a unified formalization of stochastic And-Or grammars that is agnostic to the type of data being modeled and propose an unsupervised approach to learning both the structure and parameters of such grammars.
The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the proposed approach optimizes the posterior probability of the grammar, which is a well-defined objective function. Secondly, the authors induce compositions and reconfigurations in a unified manner, which makes the approach more efficient and robust. The experimental results show that the proposed approach achieves satisfactory performance in learning event grammars and image grammars, outperforming previous approaches in some cases.
However, there are also some weaknesses in the paper. Firstly, the learning algorithm is heuristic, and there is no formal guarantee of correctness or convergence. Secondly, the optimization method used is also heuristic and lacks a clear connection to the optimization of the proposed energy function. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more detailed analysis of the time and space complexity of the proposed approach.
