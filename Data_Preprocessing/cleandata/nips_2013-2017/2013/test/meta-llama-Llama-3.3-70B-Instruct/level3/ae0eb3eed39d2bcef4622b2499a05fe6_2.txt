This paper introduces a novel approach to visual concept learning, which involves learning a visual concept from a small number of positive examples. The authors combine machine vision systems with Bayesian generalization models to determine the appropriate level of abstraction for a given set of visual examples. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and understandable explanation of their approach.
The strengths of the paper include the introduction of a new challenge task for computer vision, visual concept learning, and the creation of a large-scale dataset for evaluating this task. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of their results, including a comparison with human performance and various baseline algorithms. The use of Bayesian generalization models to infer the scope of generalization for a novel concept is a significant contribution, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through convincing results on large datasets.
One minor point of concern is the assignment of first clusters, which could be explored further in future work. A more significant concern is the expense of sending data between processing units in distributed algorithms, which could be a limitation of the approach. However, the authors provide correctness proofs for their algorithms and evaluate them on small synthetic datasets and larger experiments using Spark on Amazon AWS, which demonstrates the scalability of their approach.
The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work. The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.
In terms of originality, the paper introduces a novel combination of machine vision and Bayesian generalization models, which is a significant contribution to the field. The related work is adequately referenced, and the authors demonstrate how their approach differs from previous contributions.
