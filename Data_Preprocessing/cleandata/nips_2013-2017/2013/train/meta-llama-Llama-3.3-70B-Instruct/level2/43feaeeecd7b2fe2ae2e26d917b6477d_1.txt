This paper proposes novel graphical models for count-valued data, addressing the limitations of existing Poisson graphical models which can only capture negative conditional dependencies. The authors introduce three new approaches: the Truncated Poisson Graphical Model (TPGM), the Quadratic Poisson Graphical Model (QPGM), and the Sublinear Poisson Graphical Model (SPGM). These models can capture both positive and negative conditional dependencies between count-valued variables.
The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and thorough introduction to the background and motivation of the problem. The technical contributions are sound, and the authors provide rigorous proofs and theoretical analysis to support their claims. The numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed models in recovering network structures from count-valued data.
The strengths of the paper include:
* The authors address a significant limitation of existing Poisson graphical models and propose novel solutions that can capture a wider range of dependencies.
* The technical contributions are rigorous and well-supported by theoretical analysis and proofs.
* The numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed models in practice.
The weaknesses of the paper include:
* The paper is quite long and dense, which may make it challenging for some readers to follow.
* Some of the notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to readers without a strong background in graphical models and exponential families.
