This paper presents a comprehensive study on flat and hierarchical classification strategies in the context of large-scale taxonomies. They also introduce a well-founded way to select nodes that should be pruned in a taxonomy to derive a better-suited classification problem.
The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, followed by a detailed presentation of their theoretical developments. The experimental results are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed pruning strategy.

* The authors provide a theoretical explanation for the performance of flat and hierarchical classification strategies in large-scale taxonomies, which is a significant contribution to the field.
