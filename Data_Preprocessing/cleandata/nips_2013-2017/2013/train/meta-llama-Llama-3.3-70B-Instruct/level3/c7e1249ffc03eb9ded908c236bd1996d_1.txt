This paper presents a novel approach to demand estimation in multi-heterogeneous agents by introducing generalized random utility models (GRUM) and providing RJMCMC algorithms for parameter estimation. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed model through experimental results on synthetic and real datasets, showcasing its potential in demand estimation. The paper provides a comprehensive and solid work, including a detailed algorithm, experiments, and non-trivial theoretical analysis.
The strengths of the paper include its ability to tackle the problem of demand estimation in multi-heterogeneous agents, which has great practical value in studying underlying preference distributions. The introduction of GRUM and the provision of RJMCMC algorithms for parameter estimation are significant contributions to the field. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model, and the theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for the approach.
However, there are some limitations to the paper. The authors are suggested to clarify the relation of their work with the original GRUM models and include a more detailed analysis of identifiability in the related literature section. Additionally, the experiments can be improved by enlarging the scale, such as increasing the feature numbers (K and L), to provide more comprehensive verification of the new model's effectiveness.
In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of the work. The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and adequately informs the reader.
The originality of the paper lies in its novel approach to demand estimation in multi-heterogeneous agents using GRUM and RJMCMC algorithms. The paper provides a unique combination of familiar techniques and addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The significance of the paper is evident in its potential to advance the state of the art in demand estimation and provide unique insights into underlying preference distributions.
Arguments pro acceptance:
* The paper tackles a significant problem in demand estimation with great practical value.
* The introduction of GRUM and RJMCMC algorithms for parameter estimation is a significant contribution to the field.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model.
* The theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for the approach.
Arguments con acceptance:
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of identifiability in the related literature section.
* The experiments can be improved by enlarging the scale to provide more comprehensive verification of the new model's effectiveness.
* The relation of the work with the original GRUM models could be clarified.
Overall, the paper is well-written, technically sound, and provides a significant contribution to the field of demand estimation. With some minor revisions to address the limitations, the paper is deserving of acceptance.
