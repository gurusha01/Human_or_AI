This paper proposes a novel approach to image encoding based on a generative model with non-linear feature combinations and explicit position encoding. The model is designed to capture occlusions and invariances in image patches, which is a crucial aspect of visual perception. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on both artificial and natural image data, and show that it can learn meaningful component representations and receptive fields.
The paper is well-written and tackles an important problem in computer vision. The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of their model and the inference algorithm used to learn the model parameters. The experimental results are also well-presented and demonstrate the potential of the approach.
One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to capture occlusions and invariances in a probabilistic manner. The model uses a non-linear combination of features and positions, which allows it to represent complex visual scenes in a more realistic way. The authors also demonstrate that their approach can learn meaningful receptive fields, which is an important aspect of visual perception.
However, there are some areas where the paper could be improved. One of the limitations of the paper is the lack of comparison with other existing methods. The authors mention that their approach is related to convolutional neural networks, but they do not provide a direct comparison with these methods. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more detailed analysis of the learned component representations and receptive fields.
Another area for improvement is the clarity of the notation and the explanation of the mathematical derivations. Some of the equations and formulas are not clearly explained, which can make it difficult for readers to follow the paper. The authors could also provide more intuition and interpretation of the results, which would help to make the paper more accessible to a broader audience.

The approach proposed in the paper has the potential to be used in a variety of applications, such as object recognition, image segmentation, and visual perception.
