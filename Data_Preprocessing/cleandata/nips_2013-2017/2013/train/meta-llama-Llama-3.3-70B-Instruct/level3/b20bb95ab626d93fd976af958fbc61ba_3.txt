This paper proposes a novel approach to estimating sparse precision matrices using the CLIME estimator, which is a linear program that minimizes the l1 norm of the precision matrix subject to a constraint on the infinity norm of the sample covariance matrix times the precision matrix minus the identity matrix. The authors develop an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) algorithm to solve this linear program, which can be parallelized and distributed across multiple cores or machines.
The paper makes several key contributions. First, it establishes convergence rates for the objective function and optimality conditions of the ADMM algorithm. Second, it proposes a scalable distributed framework for solving the CLIME estimator, which can handle millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters. Third, it demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach through extensive experiments on both synthetic and real datasets.
The strengths of the paper include its clear and well-organized presentation, its thorough analysis of the convergence properties of the ADMM algorithm, and its impressive experimental results. The paper also provides a detailed comparison with existing methods, including CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC, and demonstrates the superiority of the proposed approach in terms of scalability and computational efficiency.
However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement. One concern is that the paper assumes a relatively simple model for the precision matrix, which may not be realistic in practice. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more discussion on the choice of hyperparameters, such as the block size and the number of iterations, and how these affect the performance of the algorithm. Finally, the paper could provide more insight into the computational complexity of the proposed approach and how it compares to existing methods.
In terms of the conference guidelines, the paper meets the criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance. The paper is technically sound, well-written, and easy to follow.
