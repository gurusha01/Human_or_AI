This paper proposes a novel approach to decision tree learning, called decision jungles, which utilizes ensembles of rooted decision directed acyclic graphs (DAGs) to achieve compact and accurate classification models. The authors highlight the limitation of traditional decision trees, which grow exponentially in memory with depth, and demonstrate how decision jungles can overcome this issue.
The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear introduction to the problem, related work, and the proposed approach. The authors provide a detailed explanation of the decision jungle framework, including the optimization algorithms used to learn the DAGs. The experimental evaluation is thorough, with comparisons to standard decision forests and other baseline methods on several classification tasks.
The strengths of the paper include:
* The proposal of a novel approach to decision tree learning, which addresses the limitation of traditional decision trees.
* The thorough experimental evaluation, which demonstrates the effectiveness of decision jungles in terms of memory efficiency and generalization.
* The clear and well-structured presentation of the paper.
However, there are some weaknesses and open questions:
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the time complexity of the proposed algorithms, as well as a comparison to other state-of-the-art methods.
* The authors mention that the approach can be extended to regression tasks, but this is not explored in the paper.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential applications of decision jungles.
Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a novel and interesting approach to decision tree learning. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of decision jungles. However, there are some open questions and areas for further research, which the authors acknowledge and plan to address in future work.
Arguments pro acceptance:
* The paper proposes a novel approach to decision tree learning, which addresses a significant limitation of traditional decision trees.
* The experimental evaluation is thorough and demonstrates the effectiveness of decision jungles.
* The paper is well-structured and easy to follow.
Arguments con acceptance:
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the time complexity of the proposed algorithms.
* The authors do not explore the extension of the approach to regression tasks.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential applications of decision jungles.
