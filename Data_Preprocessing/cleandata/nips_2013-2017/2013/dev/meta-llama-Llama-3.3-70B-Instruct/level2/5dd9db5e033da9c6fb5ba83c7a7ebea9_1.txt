This paper presents a novel approach to submodular function minimization, a fundamental problem in machine learning, computer vision, and signal processing. The authors propose a method that exploits the decomposability of submodular functions, allowing for efficient optimization procedures. The main claim of the paper is that the proposed method, based on a best approximation problem, outperforms existing approaches in terms of speed and accuracy.
The paper is well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to submodular functions and their applications, as well as a thorough review of existing methods for submodular function minimization. The proposed method is based on a proximal problem, which is shown to be equivalent to the original submodular function minimization problem. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the dual problem and its relationship to the primal problem.
The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, which is compared to existing approaches such as subgradient methods and Frank-Wolfe methods. The results show that the proposed method converges faster and is more accurate than the existing methods. The authors also demonstrate the parallelization capabilities of their approach, which can lead to significant speedups.
The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical foundations. The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the results.

* The proposed method is novel and effective, outperforming existing approaches in terms of speed and accuracy.
* The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to submodular functions and their applications.
* The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
The main weaknesses of the paper are:
