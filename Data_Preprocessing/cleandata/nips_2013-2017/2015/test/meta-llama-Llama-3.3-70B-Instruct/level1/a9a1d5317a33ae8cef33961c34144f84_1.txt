This paper introduces two new algorithms for probabilistic prediction, Inductive Venn-Abers Predictors (IVAPs) and Cross-Venn-Abers Predictors (CVAPs), which are designed to produce well-calibrated probability predictions. The authors demonstrate that IVAPs are automatically perfectly calibrated, while CVAPs, which are built on top of IVAPs using cross-validation, exhibit good empirical performance. The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the algorithms, including proofs of their validity and computational efficiency.
The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the algorithms and their properties. The empirical studies demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms, showing that they outperform existing calibration methods, such as Platt's method and isotonic regression, in terms of log loss and Brier loss.

* The introduction of two new algorithms for probabilistic prediction, which are theoretically well-founded and empirically effective.

The weaknesses of the paper include:
