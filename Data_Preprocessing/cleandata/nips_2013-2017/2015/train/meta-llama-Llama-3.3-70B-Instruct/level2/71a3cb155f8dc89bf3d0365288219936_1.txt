This paper proposes a sparse tensor graphical model for estimating the conditional independence structure within high-dimensional tensor-valued data. The authors assume that the data follow a tensor normal distribution with a Kronecker product structure of covariance, which facilitates the estimation of the precision matrix corresponding to each way of the tensor. The penalized maximum likelihood estimation of this model involves minimizing a non-convex objective function, and the authors propose an alternating minimization algorithm to solve this problem.
The main claims of the paper are: (1) the proposed algorithm achieves an estimator with the optimal statistical rate of convergence, (2) the estimator achieves estimation consistency with only one tensor sample, and (3) the algorithm is computationally efficient and outperforms existing methods in simulations.
The support for these claims is provided through theoretical analysis and numerical experiments. The authors prove that the proposed algorithm attains an estimator with the optimal statistical rate of convergence in Frobenius norm, and that the estimator achieves estimation consistency even with only one tensor sample. They also conduct extensive simulations to evaluate the numerical performance of the proposed algorithm and compare it with existing methods.
