This paper proposes a novel approach to robust estimation of sparse Gaussian Graphical Models (GGMs) in the presence of outliers. The authors introduce the Trimmed Graphical Lasso method, which guards against outliers by implicitly trimming a portion of the observations. The method is shown to be statistically consistent and provides robust estimates of the GGM parameters.
The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and their approach. The technical sections are also well-organized, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the statistical properties of their estimator.
The main contributions of the paper are:
1. The introduction of the Trimmed Graphical Lasso method, which provides a robust approach to estimating sparse GGMs in the presence of outliers.
2. The provision of statistical guarantees for the estimator, including consistency and error bounds.
3. The demonstration of the method's effectiveness through simulations and an application to yeast gene expression data.
The paper has several strengths, including:
1. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and their approach.
2. The technical sections are well-organized, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the statistical properties of their estimator.
3. The simulations and application demonstrate the method's effectiveness in practice.
However, there are some potential weaknesses and areas for improvement:
1. The paper assumes that the outliers are independent of the true data-generating process, which may not always be the case in practice.
