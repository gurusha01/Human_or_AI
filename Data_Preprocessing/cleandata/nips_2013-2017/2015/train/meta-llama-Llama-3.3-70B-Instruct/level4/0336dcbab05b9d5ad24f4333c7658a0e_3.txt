This paper presents substantial and intricate content, and its overall quality is enhanced by clear and effective writing. The experimental results are likewise commendable.
One potential improvement could be to provide a more detailed justification for the selected loss function, offering additional context or rationale behind this choice.
Additionally, a minor error was noted on page 3, where the phrase "globally optimal leads to" could be refined by removing the word "optimal" for better clarity. Overall, this is a compelling paper that tackles an engaging problem. It explores the concept of learning in relation to the top-k loss, a scenario in which k predictions are generated, and the loss is considered zero if the actual class is among the k predicted classes.
