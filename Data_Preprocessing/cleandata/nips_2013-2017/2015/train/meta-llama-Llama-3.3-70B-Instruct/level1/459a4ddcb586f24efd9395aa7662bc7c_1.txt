This paper introduces a novel Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) scheme for posterior sampling in Bayesian nonparametric mixture models with priors belonging to the general Poisson-Kingman class. The authors propose a hybrid approach that combines the strengths of conditional and marginal samplers, allowing for a more efficient and comprehensive representation of the random probability measure.
The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a thorough introduction to the background and related work. The authors provide a detailed explanation of the Poisson-Kingman processes and their relationship to the usual stick-breaking construction. The hybrid sampler is well-motivated and described, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the complete conditionals and the MCMC scheme.
The paper's strengths include its originality, as it proposes a new approach to inference in Bayesian nonparametric mixture models. The authors also provide a thorough comparison with existing marginal and conditional MCMC samplers, demonstrating the efficiency and effectiveness of their hybrid approach. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide sufficient details to allow for reproduction of the results.
However, there are some weaknesses to consider. The paper assumes a high level of background knowledge in Bayesian nonparametrics and MCMC methods, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow. Additionally, the authors could provide more discussion on the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future research.

The paper is also clear and well-organized, making it easy to follow. The originality of the paper is high, as it proposes a new approach to inference in Bayesian nonparametric mixture models.
