This paper presents a novel approach to learning Continuous-Time Hidden Markov Models (CT-HMMs) using Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithms. The authors address the challenge of efficient parameter learning in CT-HMMs, which is essential for modeling disease progression from irregularly-sampled clinical measurements. The proposed method leverages existing literature on continuous-time Markov chain (CTMC) models and extends it to CT-HMMs.
The paper is well-structured, and the authors provide a clear introduction to the problem, related work, and their approach. The technical sections are detailed and easy to follow, with pseudocode provided for the EM algorithm and the Expm method. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on two real-world datasets, glaucoma and Alzheimer's disease, and show that it outperforms a state-of-the-art method for glaucoma prediction.
The strengths of the paper include:
* The authors provide a comprehensive framework for efficient EM-based parameter learning in CT-HMMs, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The proposed approach is evaluated on two real-world datasets, demonstrating its practical value for longitudinal disease modeling and prediction.
* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide clear explanations of the technical details.
The weaknesses of the paper include:
* The paper assumes a basic understanding of CTMCs and CT-HMMs, which may make it challenging for readers without prior knowledge of these topics to follow.
* The experimental results are limited to two datasets, and it would be beneficial to evaluate the approach on additional datasets to further demonstrate its generalizability.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach and potential avenues for future research.
Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed approach is a significant contribution to the field of CT-HMM learning. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach, and the paper provides a clear and comprehensive framework for efficient EM-based parameter learning in CT-HMMs.
Arguments pro acceptance:
