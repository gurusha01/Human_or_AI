This paper presents a method for statistical computations with persistence diagrams, a summary representation of topological features in data. The authors introduce a universal kernel for persistence diagrams, which enables the use of this topological summary representation in the framework of embedding probability measures into reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces. The kernel is constructed by modifying a previously introduced kernel, and its universality is proven using a theorem from the literature.
The paper is well-written and clearly explains the background and methodology. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and position their contribution within the existing literature. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed kernel in two-sample hypothesis testing on synthetic and real-world data.
The strengths of the paper include the introduction of a universal kernel for persistence diagrams, which is a significant contribution to the field of topological data analysis. The authors also provide a clear and concise explanation of the methodology and experimental results.
However, there are some weaknesses in the paper. The experimental results, while demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed kernel, do not provide a clear comparison with existing methods. The authors could have included more detailed comparisons with other kernels or methods to demonstrate the superiority of their approach. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more discussion on the choice of parameters, such as the scale of the kernel, and how they affect the results.
