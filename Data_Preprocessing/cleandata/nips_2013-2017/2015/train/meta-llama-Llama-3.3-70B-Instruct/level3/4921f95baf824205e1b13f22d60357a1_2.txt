This paper studies the problem of estimating an unknown signal from non-linear measurements using the Generalized LASSO algorithm. The authors provide a precise analysis of the algorithm's performance in the asymptotic regime, where the number of measurements and the dimension of the signal grow large. The main contribution of the paper is a precise evaluation of the limit of the squared error between the estimated signal and the true signal, which is shown to be equal to the unique solution of a convex optimization program.
The paper is well-written and provides a clear overview of the problem and the main results. The authors also provide a detailed discussion of the relevant literature and highlight the contributions of their work. The technical proofs are deferred to the appendix, which makes the paper easier to follow for non-experts.
The strengths of the paper include:
* The authors provide a precise analysis of the Generalized LASSO algorithm, which is a popular method for estimating sparse signals.
* The paper provides a clear and concise overview of the main results, making it easy to understand for non-experts.
* The authors provide a detailed discussion of the relevant literature, highlighting the contributions of their work.
The weaknesses of the paper include:
* The paper assumes that the measurement matrix has i.i.d. Gaussian entries, which may not be realistic in all applications.
* The paper does not provide any numerical experiments to validate the theoretical results.
* The paper does not discuss the computational complexity of the algorithm, which may be an issue in practice.
Overall, the paper provides a significant contribution to the field of signal processing and machine learning, and the results have the potential to be useful in a wide range of applications. However, the paper could be improved by providing more numerical experiments and discussing the computational complexity of the algorithm.
