This paper presents a novel approach to statistical computations with persistence diagrams, a summary representation of topological features in data. The authors introduce a universal kernel for persistence diagrams, which enables the use of this topological summary representation in the framework of embedding probability measures into reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the background material and the proposed approach.
The main contribution of the paper is the proof of universality of a variant of the persistence scale space kernel, which is a necessary condition for a well-founded use in the context of probability measure embeddings. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the universal kernel in two-sample hypothesis testing on synthetic and real-world data. The experiments show promising results, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of the implications of their findings.
The paper complements previous results on learnability and generalization, finding necessary and sufficient conditions for generalization rather than learnability. The authors provide a clear and concise overview of the related work, and their approach is closely related to work in the field of kernel-based learning techniques.
One of the strengths of the paper is the clarity of the writing and the organization of the material. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the background material, and the proposed approach is well-motivated and clearly explained. The paper is also well-structured, with a clear separation between the introduction, background, methodology, experiments, and discussion.
However, one of the weaknesses of the paper is the lack of depth in discussing the implications of the results. While the authors provide a thorough discussion of the experiments and the results, they could have provided more insight into the potential applications and limitations of their approach. Additionally, the paper could have benefited from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach and its scalability to large datasets.
