This paper presents a well-written and clear contribution to the field of decentralized estimation in networks, specifically focusing on the computation of U-statistics. The authors propose new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms that efficiently compute U-statistics, which are essential in describing global properties of a statistical population.

The authors provide a detailed proof of the convergence of their algorithms, including a novel reformulation of the problem using "phantom nodes". The theoretical results are supported by numerical experiments, which demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms over existing approaches.
The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear introduction to the problem and related work. The authors provide a thorough review of existing gossip algorithms and their limitations, motivating the need for new approaches. The presentation of the algorithms and their analysis is clear and concise, making it easy for readers to understand the technical contributions.
The originality of the paper lies in the proposal of new algorithms for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The authors extend the standard estimation problem for sparse precision matrix to the case of U-statistics, providing a novel combination of familiar techniques. The use of "phantom nodes" in the analysis is also a new contribution, allowing for a more efficient and scalable approach to decentralized estimation.
The significance of the paper is high, as it addresses a difficult problem in decentralized estimation and provides a better solution than existing approaches. The results have important implications for various applications, including sensor networks, peer-to-peer networks, and social media analysis. The paper provides a unique theoretical and pragmatic approach to decentralized estimation, advancing the state of the art in this field.
In terms of quality, clarity, originality, and significance, this paper meets all the criteria for a high-quality contribution to the field. The authors are careful and honest in evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work, providing a thorough discussion of the limitations and potential extensions of their approach.
