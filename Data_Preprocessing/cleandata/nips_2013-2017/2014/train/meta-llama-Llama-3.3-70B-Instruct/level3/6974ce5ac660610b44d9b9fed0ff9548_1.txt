This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of causal inference, specifically in the context of transportability, which refers to the problem of transferring causal knowledge from one domain to another. The authors provide a complete characterization of the conditions under which causal effects can be transported from multiple source domains to a target domain, a problem known as mz-transportability.
The paper is well-written, and the authors have made a considerable effort to make the theoretical ideas accessible to a broad audience. The introduction provides a clear motivation for the problem, and the background section provides a thorough overview of the relevant concepts and previous work in the field.
The main contribution of the paper is the development of a graphical condition, called an mz∗-shedge, which characterizes the boundaries of the class of mz-transportable relations. The authors show that the existence of an mz∗-shedge precludes mz-transportability, and they provide a complete algorithm, called TRmz, for recognizing mz∗-shedges. The algorithm is shown to be complete, meaning that it can identify all instances of mz-transportability.
The paper also establishes the completeness of the do-calculus for establishing mz-transportability, which means that failure to find a proof using the do-calculus implies the non-existence of a transport formula.
