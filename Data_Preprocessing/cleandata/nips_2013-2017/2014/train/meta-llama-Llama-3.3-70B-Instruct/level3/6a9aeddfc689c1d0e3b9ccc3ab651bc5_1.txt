This paper proposes a novel approach to linear regression by considering the influence of a point, which is a new method for down-weighting regression. The authors introduce a probabilistic model to illustrate the estimator, but it is not the main focus of the paper. The paper's use of scoring functions and down-weighting points by influence has connections to existing literature on robust regression and errors in variables.
The paper's approach can be seen as using a different regression function for "large observations" than for "small observations". The authors suggest testing this by interacting the influence variable with the predictor variables. However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough exploration of the extensive literature on robust regression and errors in variables to find connections to the paper's methods and to support the claim that the approach is new.
One potential issue with the paper is that it is unclear whether the authors are truly the first to suggest weighting by influence. Similar ideas may have been proposed in earlier literature, and the authors should investigate this further. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a comparison line for the accuracy of the Maximum Likelihood Estimator (MLE) for the probabilistic model to provide a more useful benchmark.

However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough exploration of the literature and a clearer explanation of the novelty of the approach.
