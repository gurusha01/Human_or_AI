This paper proposes a framework for causal strategic inference in microfinance markets, modeling the interactions between microfinance institutions (MFIs) and villages as a game. The authors demonstrate the existence of a market-clearing equilibrium and apply their framework to predict market performance under various interventions, such as removing a loss-making bank from the market or imposing an interest rate cap.
The paper's main contribution is the equilibrium analysis for microfinance, which is a novel application of game-theoretic concepts to this field. Although similar analysis exists in other fields like paid search auctions, the authors' approach is tailored to the specific characteristics of microfinance markets.
The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the authors provide a clear and well-structured framework for analyzing microfinance markets, which is easy to follow. Secondly, they demonstrate the applicability of their framework through empirical studies using data from Bolivia and Bangladesh. The results show that their model can closely approximate the observed data and provide insights into the effects of various interventions.
However, the paper also has some weaknesses. One major issue is the poor readability and flow of the paper, which makes it difficult to understand the authors' arguments and results. The introduction section is particularly unclear, and the transition between different sections is not smooth. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the authors' methodology and more rigorous analysis of the results.
