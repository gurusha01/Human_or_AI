This paper proposes a framework for causal strategic inference in networked microfinance economies, which is a novel combination of familiar techniques from game theory and econometrics. The authors model a microfinance market as a two-sided market consisting of microfinance institutions (MFIs) and villages, and design algorithms for computing equilibria and learning model parameters. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the model, including the existence and uniqueness of equilibrium points, and presents an empirical study based on microfinance data from Bangladesh and Bolivia.
The strengths of the paper include its originality, technical soundness, and clarity. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their framework, and the empirical study is well-designed and executed. The paper also addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research, and provides unique insights into the microfinance market.
However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement. The paper assumes that the quality of the experts is not given and must be estimated from data, but it is not clear how the authors plan to estimate the quality of the experts. The significance of the paper is high, as it provides a novel framework for analyzing microfinance markets and evaluating the effects of interventions. The paper also has the potential to inform policy decisions and improve the functioning of microfinance markets.
