This paper presents a novel approach to denoising a highly noisy sensor network using local best-response dynamics, allowing for fast learning from a small number of label queries. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the denoising power of various dynamics, including synchronous, asynchronous, and random-order updates. The paper also presents experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach in denoising the sensor network and learning the target boundary.
The strengths of the paper include:
* A clear and well-motivated problem statement, addressing a significant challenge in machine learning and sensor networks.
* A thorough and rigorous analysis of the denoising power of various dynamics, providing valuable insights into the behavior of the system.
* Experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach, including a comparison with active and passive learning algorithms.
However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement:
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with previous work on active learning and denoising, particularly in the context of sensor networks.
* The authors assume a uniform distribution of sensors, which may not always be the case in practice. It would be interesting to explore the robustness of the approach to different distributions.
* The paper focuses primarily on linear separators, and it would be useful to extend the results to more general decision boundaries.
In terms of the conference guidelines, the paper scores well on:
* Quality: The paper is technically sound, with a clear and well-motivated problem statement, and a thorough analysis of the denoising power of various dynamics.
* Clarity: The paper is well-written, with clear explanations of the proposed approach and the experimental results.

* Significance: The paper addresses a significant challenge in machine learning and sensor networks, and the results have the potential to impact a wide range of applications.
Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper, with some revisions to address the areas for improvement mentioned above.
