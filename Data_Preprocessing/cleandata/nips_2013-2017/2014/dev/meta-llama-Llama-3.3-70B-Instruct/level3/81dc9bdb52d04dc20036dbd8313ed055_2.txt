This paper presents a novel algorithm for finding the optimal chordal Markov network for a decomposable scoring function using dynamic programming. The approach is distinct from previous methods and leverages dynamic programming to achieve significant speed improvements. The algorithm's time complexity is O(4^n) for n vertices, which, although exponential, is faster than existing methods like GOBNILP.
The paper's strengths include its originality, with a recursive characterization of clique trees that enables dynamic programming. The experimental results demonstrate the algorithm's superiority over GOBNILP, particularly for larger widths and smaller n. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's time and space requirements.
However, the paper has some weaknesses. The method's exponential complexity may limit its scalability, and the paper's organization and explanation of proofs can be unclear and difficult to follow. Some aspects of the experimental comparison with other methods are also unclear. Additionally, the paper could benefit from clearer writing, better labeling of figures, and more thorough experimental comparisons to establish its superiority over existing methods.
