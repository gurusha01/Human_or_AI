This paper presents a novel framework for designing parameter-free algorithms for online linear optimization (OLO) and learning with expert advice (LEA). The authors introduce a new interpretation of parameter-free algorithms as coin betting algorithms, which reveals a common hidden structure between previous parameter-free algorithms for OLO and LEA. The framework is based on reductions to betting on outcomes of adversarial coins, and it provides a natural way for the analysis and design of online learning algorithms.
The paper's main claims are that the proposed framework provides a unified and intuitive way to design parameter-free algorithms for OLO and LEA, and that the resulting algorithms achieve optimal worst-case regret bounds. The authors support these claims through theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations.
