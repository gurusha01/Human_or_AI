This paper proposes a novel classification algorithm for estimating posterior distributions from positive-unlabeled data, with a focus on robustness to noise in the positive labels and effectiveness in high-dimensional data. The authors build upon previous work in this area, addressing the limitations of existing algorithms that often perform poorly on real-world data. The main claim of the paper is the development of two practical classification algorithms that explicitly model the noise in the positive labels and utilize univariate transforms built on discriminative classifiers.
The paper is well-supported by theoretical analysis, and the authors provide a clear explanation of how their approach differs from previous contributions. The use of univariate transforms to preserve the class prior is a novel aspect of the work, and the authors provide a thorough justification for this approach. The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow for an expert reader.
The strengths of the paper include its ability to address a difficult problem in a better way than previous research, and its potential to advance the state of the art in robust classification algorithms for positive-unlabeled data. The authors also provide a comprehensive review of related work, demonstrating a good understanding of the field.
However, one potential weakness of the paper is the lack of experimental results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms on real-world data. While the theoretical development is sound, it is unclear how the algorithms will perform in practice. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the approach and potential avenues for future work.
