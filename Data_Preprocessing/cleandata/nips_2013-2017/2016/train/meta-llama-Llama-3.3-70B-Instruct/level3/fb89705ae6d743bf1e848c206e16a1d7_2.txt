This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of contextual semibandits, a problem that arises in various applications such as personalized search, recommendation, and crowdsourcing. The authors develop two oracle-based algorithms, VCEE and EELS, for contextual semibandits with known and unknown weights, respectively. 
The paper is well-structured, and the authors provide a clear overview of the problem, related work, and their contributions. The technical sections are detailed and well-explained, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms.
The strengths of the paper include the development of efficient algorithms for contextual semibandits, which is a challenging problem. The authors provide regret bounds for their algorithms, which are comparable to or better than existing results. The experimental evaluation is also a significant strength, as it demonstrates the practical effectiveness of the proposed algorithms.
One potential weakness of the paper is that the authors assume access to a supervised learning oracle, which may not always be available in practice. However, the authors justify this assumption by noting that supervised learning oracles have been used as a computational primitive in many settings.
Another potential weakness is that the paper focuses on the stochastic setting, and it is unclear how the algorithms would perform in the adversarial setting. However, the authors note that their algorithms can be extended to the adversarial setting, and they provide some discussion on this topic.
In terms of originality, the paper makes significant contributions to the field of contextual semibandits. The development of efficient algorithms for this problem is a notable achievement, and the experimental evaluation demonstrates the practical effectiveness of these algorithms.
