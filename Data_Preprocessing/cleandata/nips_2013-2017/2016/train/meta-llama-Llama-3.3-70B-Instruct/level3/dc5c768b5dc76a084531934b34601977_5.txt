This paper proposes a novel approach to learning conditional distributions using a conditional generative moment-matching network (CGMMN). The authors extend the concept of maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) to conditional distributions, introducing a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The CGMMN framework is designed to learn a flexible conditional distribution given some input variables, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.
The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear introduction to the background and motivation of the work. The technical sections are detailed and easy to follow, with a good balance between theoretical derivations and practical implementations. The experimental results are impressive, showing competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods in several tasks.
One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to handle complex conditional distributions using a simple and efficient training process. The authors demonstrate the flexibility of the CGMMN framework by applying it to different tasks, including predictive modeling, data generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge. The results show that CGMMN is a powerful tool for learning conditional distributions, and its performance is comparable to or even surpasses that of other state-of-the-art methods.
However, there are some areas that could be improved. For example, the authors could provide more detailed analysis of the CMMD criterion and its relationship to other distance metrics, such as KL-divergence or Wasserstein distance. Additionally, the authors could explore more extensive experiments to demonstrate the robustness and scalability of the CGMMN framework.
In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of the material. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results, and the authors are careful to evaluate both the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.
