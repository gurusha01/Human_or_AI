This paper proposes a novel approach that integrates nonlinear likelihoods from neural networks with structured latent variables using various pre-existing tools. The authors combine the strengths of probabilistic graphical models and deep learning methods to create a general modeling and inference framework. The framework, called Structured Variational Autoencoders (SVAEs), uses graphical models for representing structured probability distributions and variational autoencoders for learning nonlinear feature models.
The paper is well-written, with helpful examples and comments on related work. The authors demonstrate the scalability of their approach through examples and accompanying videos. The use of conjugate exponential families and natural gradients improves optimization efficiency, making it a notable contribution.
The main strength of the paper is its ability to combine flexible deep learning feature models with structured Bayesian priors, allowing for the combination of discrete and continuous latent variables with rich nonlinear observations. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach, making it easy to follow and understand.
However, the reviewer's main concern is whether combining earlier tools is a sufficient novel contribution for the conference. While the paper builds upon existing work, it is not clear if the combination of these tools is novel enough to warrant acceptance. The paper's originality and impact are questionable, as it may not significantly advance the state of the art in a demonstrable way.
