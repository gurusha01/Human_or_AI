This paper proposes a novel approach to statistical inference for cluster trees, which are used to represent the hierarchy of high-density clusters in a density function. The authors develop methods for constructing and summarizing confidence sets for the unknown true cluster tree, with applications to synthetic examples and a Graft-versus-Host Disease (GvHD) data set.
The paper is well-written and clearly organized, with a thorough introduction to the problem of cluster tree inference and a detailed explanation of the proposed methods. The authors provide a comprehensive review of related work in the field, highlighting the limitations of existing approaches and the contributions of their own work.
The proposed methods are based on a partial ordering of cluster trees, which allows for the pruning of statistically insignificant features and the construction of confidence sets. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through a series of experiments on synthetic data and a real-world data set.
One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity and readability. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the technical details, making it easy for non-experts to follow. The paper is also well-organized, with a logical flow of ideas and a clear structure.
However, one of the weaknesses of the paper is the lack of comparative methods. The authors do not provide a comparison of their approach with existing methods, which makes it difficult to evaluate the performance of their approach relative to others. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach and potential avenues for future research.
