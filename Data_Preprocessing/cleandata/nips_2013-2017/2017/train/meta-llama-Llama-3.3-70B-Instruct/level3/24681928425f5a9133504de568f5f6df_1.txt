This paper presents a new algorithm, Multiplicative Update Graph Matching (MPGM), for solving the Quadratic Programming (QP) matching problem with doubly stochastic and discrete constraints. The algorithm is based on a multiplicative update technique and has three main benefits: it solves the general QP problem with doubly stochastic constraint naturally, it can generate a sparse solution and thus incorporates the discrete constraint approximately, and it is efficient and simple to implement.
The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the algorithm and its theoretical guarantees. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of MPGM in solving graph matching problems, including synthetic data, image sequence data, and real-world image data. The results show that MPGM outperforms other state-of-the-art methods in terms of objective score and matching accuracy.
The strengths of the paper include its clear and concise presentation, its thorough theoretical analysis, and its comprehensive experimental evaluation. The algorithm is also simple to implement and has a low computational complexity.
However, there are some weaknesses to the paper. One of the main limitations is that the paper does not provide a theoretical proof of the sparsity of the MPGM solution. While the experimental results demonstrate that the solution is sparse, a theoretical guarantee would be more convincing. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including a discussion of the advantages and disadvantages of each method.
In terms of the NIPS conference guidelines, the paper meets the criteria for quality, clarity, and significance.
