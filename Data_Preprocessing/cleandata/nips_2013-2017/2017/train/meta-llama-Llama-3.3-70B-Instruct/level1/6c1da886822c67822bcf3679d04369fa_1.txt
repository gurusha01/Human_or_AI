This paper introduces Masked Autoregressive Flow (MAF), a novel approach to density estimation that combines the strengths of autoregressive models and normalizing flows. The authors demonstrate that MAF achieves state-of-the-art performance in a range of general-purpose density estimation tasks, outperforming existing methods such as Real NVP and MADE.
The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the background and motivation of the work. The technical contributions of the paper are significant, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the relationship between MAF and other existing methods, such as Inverse Autoregressive Flow and Real NVP.
The experimental evaluation of MAF is comprehensive, covering a range of datasets and tasks, including unconditional and conditional density estimation. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of MAF in modeling complex densities and its ability to benefit from conditional information.
The strengths of the paper include:
* The introduction of a novel and effective approach to density estimation
* A thorough analysis of the relationship between MAF and other existing methods
* Comprehensive experimental evaluation on a range of datasets and tasks
* Clear and concise writing style
The weaknesses of the paper include:
* The paper assumes a significant amount of background knowledge in density estimation and normalizing flows, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow
* Some of the experimental results, such as the comparison with Real NVP, could be more detailed and comprehensive
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the potential applications and limitations of MAF
Arguments for acceptance:

Arguments against acceptance:
