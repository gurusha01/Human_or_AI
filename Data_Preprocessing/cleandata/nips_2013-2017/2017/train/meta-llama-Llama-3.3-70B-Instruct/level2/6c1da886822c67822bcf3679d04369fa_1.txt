This paper introduces Masked Autoregressive Flow (MAF), a novel approach to density estimation that combines the strengths of autoregressive models and normalizing flows. The authors demonstrate that MAF achieves state-of-the-art performance in a range of general-purpose density estimation tasks, outperforming existing methods such as Real NVP and MADE.
The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the background and motivation of the work. The technical contributions of the paper are significant, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the relationship between MAF and existing methods such as Inverse Autoregressive Flow and Real NVP.
The experimental evaluation of MAF is comprehensive, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness on a range of datasets, including UCI datasets, image patches, and conditional density estimation tasks. The results show that MAF outperforms existing methods in most cases, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of MAF.
One of the key strengths of the paper is its clarity and readability. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the results.
