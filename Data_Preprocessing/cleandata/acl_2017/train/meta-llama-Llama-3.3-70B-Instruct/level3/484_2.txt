This paper proposes a novel approach to keyphrase prediction in scientific publications using a generative model based on the encoder-decoder framework. The main contributions of this work are: 
1. The application of the encoder-decoder model to keyphrase prediction, which allows the model to capture the deep semantic meaning of the text and generate keyphrases based on this understanding.
2. The incorporation of a copy mechanism into the model, which enables it to predict rarely-occurred phrases and handle out-of-vocabulary words.
3. The comprehensive evaluation of the proposed model on multiple datasets, including a new dataset (KP20k) that is significantly larger than existing datasets.
The strengths of this paper include the novelty of the approach, the thorough evaluation of the model, and the potential of the model to be applied to other domains and types of text. The use of the encoder-decoder framework and the copy mechanism allows the model to capture complex semantic relationships and generate high-quality keyphrases.
However, there are some weaknesses to the paper. One weakness is that the paper spends a significant amount of space explaining well-known concepts, such as the encoder-decoder model and the attention mechanism, which may not be necessary for readers familiar with these topics. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more analysis of the results and more discussion of the implications of the findings.
Some potential questions to ask the authors include: 
* How do the results of the model compare to human annotators, and what are the implications of the model's performance for real-world applications?
* How does the model handle keyphrases that are not present in the text, and what are the limitations of the model in this regard?
* What are the potential applications of the model beyond keyphrase prediction, and how could it be extended to other domains and types of text? 
Overall, this paper presents a novel and promising approach to keyphrase prediction, and the results demonstrate the potential of the model to generate high-quality keyphrases. However, further analysis and discussion are needed to fully understand the implications of the findings and to explore the potential applications of the model.
