This article addresses the issue of maintaining privacy in a ranging scenario where multiple teachers are trained on distinct sets of sensitive data that do not overlap each other and the student predicts outcomes using public data labeled by the teachers via a noisy voting system. I believe the method suggested by the authors is well explained and trustworthy. It would be helpful to delve into the limitations of Theorem 1 that were mentioned for further discussion. The writers skillfully repurpose the concept of introducing disturbance into the calculations. A well known idea in the realm of privacy protection. And use it in a much wider (non linear) and more useful setting than many previous studies on privacy and related areas have done before.The methods wide utility and clear progress beyond methods along with its clear presentation make it a compelling option, for publication.
