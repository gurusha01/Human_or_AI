This research paper presents an approach called Classless Association that aims to tackle the challenge of establishing connections between two instances of an unspecified class without depending on preset labels. Taking inspiration from the Symbol Grounding Problem and how infants learn associations this method utilizes two separate Multilayer Perceptrons trained through an Expectation Maximization technique. During training procedures the model ensures that the outputs of the networks correspond with a pattern allowing them to reach a consensus on pseudo categories, for input pairs. The researchers test their model on four datasets without classes that originate from MNIST and compare how well it works against unsupervised methods as benchmarks in the field of machine learning performance evaluation. The outcomes indicate that the suggested model surpasses unsupervised grouping techniques and delivers outcomes comparable, to supervised learning outcomes even though no labels are present.
